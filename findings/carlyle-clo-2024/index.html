01 — The Exposure

A coverage-test cascade under constrained proceeds

The Carlyle CLO 2024-1 indenture contains a Priority of Payments waterfall with multiple independent coverage-test triggers. When a coverage test fails, the waterfall directs payment through the Debt Payment Sequence. Each trigger draws from the same finite pool of available Interest Proceeds.

Validity detected that, under a stress state where multiple coverage tests fail simultaneously, the aggregate draw obligation can exceed available proceeds. Lower-priority payment obligations cannot be fully satisfied.

The waterfall resolves this by design — senior claims are paid first, junior claims receive what remains. That is how CLO structures are built to function under stress.

What Validity adds is different: a formal proof of the exact structural condition under which that shortfall occurs. Not a scenario estimate. Not a risk opinion. A deterministic proof object, produced automatically from the unmodified filing.

02 — What This Means for Your Documents

This pattern is not unique to this transaction.

Any CLO indenture that conditions Debt Payment Sequence draws on coverage test failures, without an explicit aggregate cap on simultaneous triggers, creates an analogous stress-state exposure. The specific thresholds, note classes, and fund dynamics will differ. The structural logic is the same.

The question is not whether your documents contain conditional payment logic. They do. The question is whether you have a systematic way to verify how that logic behaves under defined stress states — before the market, a trustee, or a counterparty forces you to find out.

Validity answers that question with a proof, not an estimate.

If on any Payment Date the amount available in the Payment Account is insufficient to make the full amount of the disbursements required by the Distribution Report, the Trustee shall make the disbursements called for in the order and according to the priority set forth under the Priority of Payments, subject to Section 13.1, to the extent funds are available therefor.
05 — What This Is Not

A demonstration, not an allegation

This is not a claim that the Carlyle CLO is defective or improperly drafted. The document is legally coherent. The waterfall functions as intended.

It is not a claim that rating agencies, counsel, or investors failed in their review. The exposure Validity identified is not visible to qualitative review. It is visible to formal logic.

It is a demonstration that verification infrastructure can detect and prove stress-state payment interactions that conventional document review does not express as deterministic logic.
