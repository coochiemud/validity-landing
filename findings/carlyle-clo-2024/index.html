Stress Exposure Detected
Under simultaneous coverage-test failures, the waterfall's Debt Payment Sequence draws exceed available Interest Proceeds. Lower-priority obligations cannot be satisfied.
Finding Type: Conditional UNSAT · Clauses: 5 provisions · Engine: Z3 · LFS v2.0
01 — The Finding

The Priority of Payments waterfall contains four independent coverage-test triggers — clauses (F), (H), (K), and (M) — each directing payment through the Debt Payment Sequence when a test fails. Each draw competes for the same pool of available Interest Proceeds. No provision caps the aggregate across simultaneous failures.

Under a stress state where multiple tests fail at once, the combined draw obligation exceeds available funds. Section 11.1.2 instructs the Trustee to disburse in priority order to the extent funds are available. Lower-priority obligations go unmet.

This is how the waterfall is designed to function. Validity proved the exact condition under which it happens.

§ 11.1.2 — Insufficiency of Funds
If on any Payment Date the amount available in the Payment Account is insufficient to make the full amount of the disbursements required by the Distribution Report, the Trustee shall make the disbursements called for in the order and according to the priority set forth under the Priority of Payments, subject to Section 13.1, to the extent funds are available therefor.
