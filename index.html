The Problem

Complex agreements behave like systems

Modern financial and legal agreements contain hundreds of defined terms and conditional clauses. These clauses interact to determine rights, obligations, and financial outcomes. Yet their logical structure is rarely tested.

Contradictions, circular dependencies, and impossible conditions can remain hidden until a dispute, audit, or financial loss exposes them.

Traditional document review focuses on interpretation. It does not test whether the system itself works.

The Solution

Validity verifies the logic inside agreements

Validity converts structured legal language into formal logic and evaluates whether the rules inside a document are internally consistent.

The system identifies issues such as:

Contradictory conditions
Circular dependencies
Undefined or inconsistent references
Incompatible thresholds or triggers
Clauses that cannot be satisfied simultaneously

These issues often arise from interactions across multiple clauses and are difficult to detect through manual review. Validity exposes those interactions.
