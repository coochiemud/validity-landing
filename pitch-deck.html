The Missing Layer

We have:

  • ✓ Blockchain to verify transactions
  • ✓ Audit firms to verify numbers
  • ✓ Law firms to verify contracts
  • ✓ Credit agencies to verify creditworthiness

We don't have:

  • ✗ Infrastructure to verify reasoning

We can audit numbers and compliance.

We can't audit reasoning.

The Problem

High-stakes decisions rely on documents whose internal logic is never formally verified.

Current Review Process:

  • Compliance checks format and disclosure requirements
  • Analysts review financial models and projections
  • Senior leadership reviews narrative and positioning
  • No layer tests internal logical consistency
Documents can contain mutually inconsistent claims—and those inconsistencies escape review.

Why This Matters

High-stakes decision documents contain verifiable claims:

  • Financial projections with embedded assumptions
  • Risk disclosures that may contradict operational claims
  • Regulatory assertions that may conflict with disclaimers
  • Valuation methodologies that may contradict stated principles

These claims are testable.

They are currently untested.

The Technical Challenge

Why hasn't this been done?

Rare Expertise Combination

Formal methods expertise is rare—and rarely paired with financial domain knowledge

Translation Complexity

Financial language → decidable logic fragments requires domain-specific specification

Refusal Discipline

System must refuse claims outside supported fragment—most systems don't enforce boundaries

Recent Feasibility

Claim extraction and structured NLP have matured to enterprise-grade reliability and cost

The Solution

Technical Architecture:

  • Logical Fragment Specification (LFS): Defines decidable subset of financial claims
  • Translation Layer: Financial language → formal logic
  • SMT Solver Integration: Z3 (Microsoft Research) for deterministic consistency checking and counterexample artifacts
  • Provenance Tracking: Claim → text span → proof chain
  • Refusal Enforcement: Explicit boundaries on what can be verified

This is infrastructure, not tooling.
Deterministic, reproducible, traceable.

What Makes This Hard

Three technical constraints:

1. Decidability

Can only verify claims expressible in decidable fragments—most natural language is not

2. Soundness

False positives break adoption—precision must be >95% within supported domain

3. Completeness Trade-off

Early baseline: ~25% of claims are formalizable in typical documents; the system must refuse the remainder without losing utility

These constraints are the moat.
This is not an LLM wrapper.
